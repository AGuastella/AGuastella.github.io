SparsyFed

Sparse Adaptive Federated Training

Co-first author (equal contribution with L. Sani) · ICLR 2025, Singapore
pytorch federated distributed optimization research

Summary

A federated sparse training method achieving 95% model sparsity with negligible accuracy loss and 19× communication reduction vs. dense baselines, for cross-device federated learning deployments.

Results

  • 95% model sparsity with negligible accuracy loss
  • 19× communication reduction vs. dense models
  • 200× lower weight regrowth than previous methods
  • Single hyperparameter simplicity
  • Adaptive to heterogeneous data distributions (non-IID)

Why it matters

Communication cost is the bottleneck in cross-device federated learning on mobile and edge devices — a 19× reduction is a real deployment unlock, not just a benchmark number. Sparsity also means smaller models, so cheaper inference and storage on-device. And a single hyperparameter makes the method easier to tune and operate in production, instead of adding another knob to a system that already has too many.

Tech stack

PyTorch, Flower (federated learning framework), Python, Slurm, Weights & Biases
